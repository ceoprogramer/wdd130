Yucatán

"The Yucatán Peninsula is a region in southeastern Mexico, famous for its rich Mayan history, its beautiful white-sand beaches bathed by the turquoise waters of the Caribbean Sea and the Gulf of Mexico, and its delicious gastronomy. It is a place where ancestral culture blends with modernity, offering everything from impressive archaeological sites to vibrant colonial cities like Mérida. The region is also known for its cenotes, freshwater underground sinkholes of great natural beauty and cultural significance to the Maya. Chichén Itzá is one of the most important and famous Mayan archaeological sites in the world, declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site and considered one of the New Seven Wonders of the Modern World. Located in the heart of Yucatán,"
